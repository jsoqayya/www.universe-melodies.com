A symphony we don't hear

Is it an exaggeration to say that the universe is playing a symphony? A symphony so perfect and creative that it almost pierces the heart with its grandeur?

In the hustle and bustle of our busy lives, we barely hear the faintest whisper of it. It's like standing in the middle of a great music festival and plugging our ears with our fingers, only to hear a faint echo of harmonious melodies.

That's what I wanted to do in this book, to make you feel and understand it... Removing some of the blinders, opening the closed windows in our souls, making us contemplate - even for a moment - the magnificent scene of the harmony of the universe with all its components.

Consciousness - as it seems to me now, after all this research - is not just an accidental phenomenon created by chance in our brain cells. Consciousness is the very essence of existence.

Yes! The universe is not a hollow, deaf matter. The universe is a living, conscious, sentient, sensitive, aware entity. It interacts intelligently with its surroundings, testifying every moment to the oneness and greatness of its Creator.

One early morning - with the dew still glistening on the leaves - I stood on my balcony admiring the dewdrops on the petals of a small flower. A strange question occurred to me: Are these crystalline drops conscious? Do they realize the beauty of their composition? Do they feel the sweetness of their existence?

It may seem like a silly question at first glance, but it became an obsession. It prompted me to dig into modern physics books... What I found there exceeded my expectations!

Through this book, I wanted to remove the blinders... To open hearts and eyes to a magnificent scene. From the tiny atom to the great galaxy, from the tiny cell to the complex organism, from the roaring seas to the majestic mountains... They are all singing the poem of existence.

They all beat in a single rhythm. Harmonious. Integrated. The whole of it is directed towards the Creator, the Almighty.

Between faith and science A wondrous convergence

It is strange that there is a harmony between what science whispers today and what the Qur'an proclaimed fourteen centuries ago!

I remember an evening... I was sitting with one of my colleagues under a starry sky. He suddenly said to me: "Do you know that modern physics theories are getting closer and closer to the Qur'anic conceptualization of the universe?"

I stopped stargazing and looked at him... He was completely serious.

The shift in the twentieth century was radical in physics. From the mechanical model that prevailed in the age of Newton, to the quantum model that unveiled a universe more complex, mysterious, and fascinating than we ever imagined.

A universe governed not by rigid determinism, but by multiple possibilities and possibilities.

In my journey with this book, I found that consciousness is not exclusive to humans. It is pervasive in all atoms of existence.

It manifests itself in the solid stone that appears to be rigid ... in the plant that grows towards the sun ... in the little ant that knows its way to its colony. In the little ant that knows its way to its colony... In the distant star that sends its radiation across the vastness of space.

They are all different expressions of a single consciousness that pervades everything. They are all diverse praises to the Great Creator.

As we talked about in the chapters of the book, quantum physics has revealed an astonishing world of cosmic entanglement. Particles that remain interconnected and interact, even when separated by millions of light years!

This suggests that the separation we feel in our physical world is an illusion. The deepest truth of existence is unity and interconnectedness. It is as if the whole universe is one harmonious organism, whose parts are connected to each other in an intricate web of relationships and interactions.

What I wanted to prove through this book is not only the harmony and praise of the universe, but also the deep harmony between faith and science... between revelation and reason between religion and modernity.

In recent centuries, a misconception has prevailed: That science and religion are contradictory, and that every scientific advance is a victory for materialism at the expense of faith.

This is completely wrong!

Real science, as it deepens and expands, shows us more signs of creativity in creation, and more signs of purpose and design in the universe.

The twentieth century witnessed a radical transformation in physics. From the rigid mechanical model of the Newtonian era, to the mysterious quantum model that has revealed a universe more complex, mysterious and fascinating than we ever imagined.

A universe governed not by rigid determinism, but by probability and possibility. A universe in which the observer is not separate from the observed phenomenon, but influences it by simply observing it. A universe in which particles are intertwined across time and space, as if they are aware of each other and respond to each other.

This major shift in the scientific view of the universe has revealed new dimensions in understanding consciousness and its role in existence. Whereas in the classical materialist model, consciousness was merely a secondary phenomenon resulting from chemical reactions in the brain, in the new quantum model it has become more fundamental and essential.

Some contemporary scientific theories even suggest that consciousness may be the basic fabric of existence, and that matter itself is only a manifestation of this universal consciousness.

This coincides perfectly with the Qur'anic vision, which tells us that the entire universe is harnessed to God, praising Him and following His will. A vision that places consciousness and will at the center of existence, not at its margins. It sees the universe as a manifestation of the Creator's wisdom and power, not as a deaf machine running according to blind laws.

The pulse and song of the universe

The universe is, in essence, conscious and aware. It breathes, moves, listens and responds. And perhaps more than we realize.

Once, on a visit to a nature park, I sat alone under a tall tree. I closed my eyes and tried to listen with my whole being.

Suddenly, I felt a strange pulse in the air around me. It was as if the tree was breathing, the earth was whispering, and the breeze was telling a story.

I can't describe it, but it filled me with the certainty that the outwardly silent universe is teeming with life, movement, and consciousness.

I spoke to my wife one evening about the idea of consciousness in everything, and she smiled a smile... filled with both doubt and wonder at the same time.

She said: "Do you think that a stone is aware of its existence?"

I contemplated her question for a moment, then said: "I think it possesses a form of consciousness that we cannot perceive with our limited senses. Isn't the stone composed of atoms? And at the heart of each atom lies a moving world of subatomic particles whose strange behavior quantum physics reveals to us?"

The most profound thing we explored in this book is the living pulse that runs through the veins of the universe, from the smallest atom to the largest galaxy. A pulse that carries within it the secrets of creation, the secrets of the relationship between the Creator and the created, and the secrets of the purpose of existence.

We discovered that the universe, deep down, is conscious and aware. It listens and responds, interacts and communicates. This cosmic consciousness is not separate from our human consciousness, but is connected to it, embedded in it. It is as if we are drops from the infinite sea of divine consciousness, momentarily separated from it only to return to it in the end.

Between the verses of the Qur'an and the theories of science

What struck me the most in my research journey was the amazing compatibility between what modern science reveals and what the Qur'an tells us.

Quantum physics reveals a wondrous world of cosmic entanglement, where particles remain connected and affect each other despite vast distances, as if they were communicating in an invisible language.

I was talking to a physicist one day about "quantum entanglement," and I asked him: "Isn't it strange that particles separated by worlds and universes can interact?"

He said something I didn't expect: "What we are discovering today makes me wonder... whether the entire universe is one interconnected body, and that the apparent separation between its components is an illusion?"

I paused at this answer...

In this book, we have been able to show the deep and surprising compatibility between the Qur'anic vision of the universe and the findings of modern scientific theories.

Quantum physics has revealed a strange world of cosmic entanglement, where particles communicate with each other across vast distances and are instantly affected by each other beyond the boundaries of time and space, as if they belong to a single integrated entity.

Recent biological studies have shown that living cells possess a kind of intelligence and cognition. They can sense and interact with their surroundings and make complex decisions that cannot be explained by mere random chemical reactions.

The cells communicate with each other in a complex language, and work in harmony like a musical orchestra, where each player plays his role perfectly, within an integrated tapestry of melodies and harmonies.

Recent cosmological theories have revealed an amazingly finely balanced universe, as if it were specifically designed to host life and consciousness. If any of the fundamental physical constants were to change even a tiny fraction of a percent, there would be no habitable universe. It is as if the universe already knew it would host consciousness, as if consciousness was part of the "plan" from the beginning.

All these modern scientific discoveries and theories miraculously intersect with the Quran's vision of the universe, a book that tells us that inanimate objects swim, trees prostrate, mountains bow, the sun, moon, stars and everything in the universe moves according to a tight divine order, and speaks of the oneness and greatness of the Creator.

The Journey of Research and Verification

The journey of writing this book was not easy. It took me years of research and exploration.

I remember long nights spent in front of the computer screen... Scrolling through dozens of websites and research papers... I read what physicists have come up with in their latest theories... I absorb what biologists have discovered in the behavior of cells... And what astronomers have deduced from the properties and laws of the universe.

I was moving between multiple sources like a bee between flowers, sipping the nectar of knowledge from here and there, and trying hard to weave from these scattered information an integrated fabric, linking the verses of the Qur'an with the data of modern science, honestly, accurately, and without pretense.

How many times have I tossed my papers aside and said to myself: "This research is bigger than me"!

But my longing to discover more about this wondrous universe and its praise kept me going.

I did not want to present the reader with mere self-reflections or personal impressions, but rather an integrated picture, based on solid scientific foundations and drawing on contemporary human knowledge in various fields.

I have been careful to be accurate in reporting scientific opinions and theories, to be honest in attributing statements to their owners, and to be objective in presenting different points of view.

I did not seek to adapt science to serve a preconceived idea, but rather followed the scientific method of thinking and investigation, believing that scientific truth, if reached with integrity and impartiality, cannot conflict with the truth of faith, because their source is one and the same.

I used dozens of specialized scientific sources and references and reviewed hundreds of researches and studies to compile the material for this book. My first concern was to provide accurate and reliable information that stands up to scientific criticism and is consistent with contemporary knowledge.

At the same time, I made sure to formulate this information in a literary style that addresses the heart as well as the mind, and evokes feelings of amazement and admiration in the face of the greatness of creation and the splendor of divine creativity.

Real science does not kill poetry and the sense of beauty, but rather deepens and deepens it, and opens wider and wider horizons.

Human and the Conscious Universe

At the center of this vision, what is the place of the human being?

I explain throughout the book that humans occupy a special place in the cosmic consciousness system. Not because he is superior to other creatures, but because he possesses a unique capacity for conscious choice and free will.

While all creatures praise God by their innate and inspired nature, man alone has the freedom to join or deviate from this cosmic symphony.

This freedom is the basis of his mandate and test, and is the essence of his God-given caliphate on earth.

I meditate a lot on the meaning of man being "God's successor on earth"... and I see that this meaning is much deeper than what is commonly believed.

The caliph is the one who carries the trust of the one who created him, and works according to his will and will. If the whole universe swims in the praise of God, man is required to be the leader of this symphony, aware of its harmony, contributing to its harmony, not destroying its order and disrupting its harmony.

This freedom is the center of the mandate and test, and the basis of the caliphate that God gave to man on earth. God entrusted man with the earth and made him a caliph in it, and endowed him with the knowledge and ability to enable him to build and reform it.
